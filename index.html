Abstract

I used metabolite and clinical data for 80 individuals in order to examine if there are clusters in the metabolite profile. I then analysed whether these groups associated with certain clinical parameters. I used k-means clustering for the cluster analysis and LDA for the discriminate analysis. I found three groups with regards to metabolite profiles and different clinical parameters associating with these these groups i.e.hdl, fatp, tg for cluster 3, scfat and bmi for cluster 2, iafat for cluster 1.

Final assignment using my own data

We have multivariate data: clinical and metabolite data. The aim of the study is to see if there are clear metabolite profiles in our dataset i.e.to see if our individuals cluster according to their metabolite profiles (83 metabolites). Biologically speaking, this will show if we can maindividuals into clusters using metabolite profiles. While we are at it, we want to see if these profiles manifest as different clinical profiles. In other words, can we find differences in the previously defined clusters when you look at the clinical parameters of the individuals in the clusters. T see if these clusters also show differences in clinical measures, I will use LDA to analyse the difference between the clinical measures in the groups that have been defined previsouly by metabolite measures. The data was pre-processed to exclude metabolites with NA, some clinical variables removed and some clinical variables renames.

The research question is:

  1. Do individuals fall into groups according to their genome-wide metabolites?
  2. Do these groups show differences in clinical measures as well?

This dataset has both clinical measures (9 measures) and metabolite measures (83 measures) for 80 individuals. The clinical measures show the health parameters that the individulas exhibit and were measures by a clinician. The metabolites were measured in a mass spectomertry lab using blood samples from these individuals. In essence, the data is two-fold: the clinical measures show the outer phenotype/symptoms, the metabolite measures show the molecular phenotype and measure the protein amount in the body.

The metabolites are highly co-linear.

The individuals themselves are monozygotic twins and thus share the same genotype (gene profile) as his or her co-twin.

The data are as follows: ID : individual Id batch : the batch in which the blood samples for the metabolite was run family : family ID sex : male or female age : age of individual bmi : body mass index (this has been known to be a major confounder for most metabolites) Smoking : 1= smoking, 2= non-smoking individual fatp : fat percentage liverfat : liverfat amount iafat : inter-abdominal fat amount scfat : subcutaneous fat amount kol : total cholesterol level hdl : hdl cholesterol level ldl : ldl cholesterol level tg : triglicerides level crp : inflammation level HMDB00123: HMDB00791: 83 different metabolites named according to the Human Metabolome DataBase.

There are 80 rows and 109 variables in this dataset.

All variables are numerical/integer except sex, batch and smoking which are factors.

Conclusion and discussion

In conclusion, kmeans clustering revealed that there were 3 metabolite profiles. These metabolite profiles then associated nicely with some key clinical measures like scfat, intraabdominal fat, tg and hdl. It might be useful in the future in predicting the molecular patterns just by looking at the clinical measures.

I found that personally for me it is difficult to figure our how many clusters are optimal. And since I deal with twin data (related samples), Im not sure if I should do anything differently for these methods.
